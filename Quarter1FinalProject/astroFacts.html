Some Terms Defined...

Astonomical Unit (AU)
A unit of measure which is roughly the distance from the Earth to the Sun. It is exactly equivalent to 150 million kilometers (93 million miles.)
Aphelion
The distance an orbiting body is when farthest from the central body around which it orbits.
Perihelion
The distance an orbiting body is when closest to the central body around which it orbits.
Prograde Rotation
Occurs when an orbiting body rotates in the same direction in which its central body rotates.
Retrograde Rotation
Occurs when an orbiting body rotates opposite the direction in which its central body rotates.

Did You Notice...

The orbital distances from the sun scale up quite a bit as we move further from the sun. All 4 of the inner planets are within 2 AU from the sun, but then the outer planets are separated from eachother by much greater distances of up to nearly 10 AU! Furthermore, the distances between the aphelion and perihelion of the outer planets are surprisingly big. Consider the fact that a couple of the outer planets have differences of more than 1 AU, remember that that is greater than the distance from earth to the sun! Also notice how large the rotation times for Venus and Mercury are compared to the others, which are less than or roughly equal to 24 hours. Venus' is even longer than its orbital period, making one day on Venus longer than a year there! And finally, while all planets are orbitally prograde as they all orbit in the same direction that the sun rotates, significantly two of our planets actually rotate in retrograde. There are some theories as to why Venus and Uranus are different, and Venus is especially debated, but no one knows for sure.

Something Is Missing...

Some may find that Pluto is unfortunately absent from the list of planets in our Solar System. It has been famously demoted from planet to dwarf planet in a controvertial, yet totally rational, move by the general astronomers' community. Unfortunately it fails to meet the planetary condition of being the dominant body in its orbital path as it clashes with other nearby dwarf planets like Eris for that title. Therefore it fails to be a full-time planet and is rather just a dwarf planet.
